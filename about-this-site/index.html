About this site

So, what you’re looking at is the result of a weekend hack project inspired by this post by Harper Reed back in January 2011. It outlines how he figured out how to leverage Google’s AppEngine to host his static sites for free using DryDrop. For some reason, after reading that, I kinda said, “huh, that’s neat”, and never really did anything about it. Until, early on the morning of October 22, I suddenly had the urge to write about some of the hacking projects I was working on but, other than paying for a domain, I didn’t want to bother with the nitty-gritty of managing any kind of hosted site.

So, I downloaded DryDrop, setup a vagrant VM, and got to work. What you’re reading and looking at is the result. A static site that is updated by pushing content into this github repo. I managed to get comments going though Disqus and figured that any other “interactive” elements can be easily wired in with a bit of Javascript magic. The only thing that I’m missing at the moment is some kind of search mechanism but considering there’s not much here at the moment, there’s not much need. At any rate, I’m looking at using something like this down the road. If you’re interested in how I accomplished this, here’s a post I wrote after setting it up.

Anyways, it’s not an original concept but I thought it was a pretty nice weekend hack project and maybe I’ll even start polluting the internet with some of my Python/Django/whatever musings in the coming months.
